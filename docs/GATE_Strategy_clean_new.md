# The GOLDEN GATE Strategy — Clean Edition

*A simple, complete, plain-English trading plan. Educational only — not financial advice.*

---

## 1. The Idea in One Line

Every stock moves in a rhythm: **Trend → Correction → Trend.** This strategy ignores the trend and
waits for the **correction to fully finish**. When it does, a fresh trend is born — and that birth is
where you buy.

---

## 2. How a Correction Finishes (the whole secret)

A correction is only *truly* over when **two things happen at the same place:**

1. **Price Correction** — the price falls back and **touches the 200 EMA.**
2. **Time Correction** — the four moving averages (20, 50, 100, 200 EMA) **squeeze together** into a
   tight bunch. This tight bunch is the **GATE.**

When price has touched the 200 EMA **and** the four EMAs are tightly bunched, the old move is done and
a new trend is ready to launch. Price then **shoots out of the gate** — that breakout is your entry.

> Think of the four EMAs as a closing gate. While they're spread apart, the trend is still running.
> When they shut tight and price comes back to them, the gate is "loaded." When price bursts through,
> the new trend begins.

---

## 3. Tools You Need

- **Four EMAs on the chart:** 20, 50, 100, 200.
- **Fibonacci retracement** (drawn on the last big up-move) — used only to confirm the correction is
  mature; the 0.382–0.618 zone should sit near the gate.
- **Volume** and a rough sense of **candle size (range)** — to confirm a real breakout vs a fake one.
- **Log scale** charts for big timeframes (weekly/monthly); normal scale is fine for small timeframes.

---

## 4. Timeframes — Small to Big

Always read charts **small timeframe first, then big.** A move always starts small and grows up the ladder:

```
1m → 3m → 5m → 10m → 15m → 30m → 1h → 2h → 3h → 4h → Daily → Weekly → Monthly
```

Pick the timeframe that matches your goal:

| Your goal | Trade on | Typical holding |
|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| Long-term investing | Monthly / Weekly | months to years |
| Swing trading | Daily / 4h | weeks to months |
| Short-term / positional | 1h–2h | days to weeks |
| Intraday | 5m–15m | hours |

Bigger timeframe = bigger, slower, more reliable moves. Smaller = faster, noisier.

---

## 5. The Three Bounces (so you know where you are)

After a new trend starts, corrections get progressively deeper:

- **1st correction** stops at the **20 EMA.**
- **2nd correction** stops at the **50 EMA.**
- **3rd correction** stops at the **100 EMA.**
- The **full reset** happens when price finally reaches the **200 EMA** — and that's when the next
  *major* trend begins.

(Exception: on the **monthly** chart, strong index/blue-chip stocks often bottom at the **100 EMA**
instead of the 200. For those, treat the 100 EMA as the floor.)

---

## 6. The Setup — Three Simple Checks

A valid GATE buy needs all three, in order:

**A. The Gate is shut (squeeze).**
The 20/50/100/200 EMAs are bunched tightly together. Simple test: the gap between the highest and
lowest of the four EMAs is small compared to the stock's recent normal — i.e. the EMAs are unusually
close *for this stock*, and recent candles are small/quiet (low volatility).

**B. Price has come to the floor.**
The price has pulled back and **touched (or nearly touched) the 200 EMA** (100 EMA for monthly
blue-chips). Bonus confirmation: this happens inside the Fibonacci **0.382–0.618** zone of the last
up-move.

**C. Price breaks out with force.**
Price **closes above the top of the gate** on a **bigger-than-usual candle** with **above-average
volume.** This is the contraction-to-expansion moment. Quiet squeeze, then a loud breakout.

If A + B are true but C hasn't happened yet → it's a **Watch**, not a buy. Don't anticipate.

---

## 7. Entry — Exactly When to Buy

- Buy when the breakout candle **closes above the top of the gate** (Check C), **or** place a buy order
  just above the gate's high.
- The breakout must be **convincing** (big candle + volume). A weak poke above that closes back inside
  the gate **does not count.**
- Buy in the **direction of the breakout** — up-breakout = buy, down-breakout = (short / avoid).

---

## 8. Stop Loss — Exactly Where

- Put your stop at the **200 EMA of the timeframe one step smaller** than the one you traded.
  - *Example:* you bought a **Daily** gate breakout → your stop is the **4-hour** 200 EMA.
- Place it a little below that line (small buffer) so a normal wick doesn't knock you out.
- **Never move your stop wider.** You may only tighten it as the trade moves in your favour.
- If the gap between your entry and your stop is too large (more than ~8% away on a swing trade), the
  trade is too risky — **skip it or buy fewer shares.**

---

## 9. Position Size — How Many Shares

Decide your size **from your stop**, never from a gut feeling. Risk a small fixed slice of your account
on each trade (start with **1%**):

```
Money you risk on this trade  =  Account × 1%
Risk per share                =  Entry price − Stop price
Number of shares to buy       =  (Money you risk) ÷ (Risk per share)   → round down
```

Caps that keep you safe:
- No single position bigger than ~20–25% of your account.
- Total risk across **all** open trades never more than **5%** of your account.

---

## 10. Targets — Where to Book Profit

Don't exit on a fixed number — let the move run, but take money along the way:

- Book a **first partial** at the **1.272 / 1.618 Fibonacci extension** of the breakout move.
- **Trail the rest** behind the rising **20 EMA** (or behind the next-bigger-timeframe gate once the
  move has grown up a level).
- Use the timeframe's *typical travel* below as a **rough expectation** — to stop you from selling a
  big trade after a small wiggle. These are not guarantees:

| Timeframe | Rough typical move |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Monthly | very large (multi-year) |
| Weekly | large (1–3 years) |
| Daily | ~50–70% over months |
| 4h | ~35–40% |
| 1h–2h | ~20–30% |
| 15m–30m | ~15–20% |
| 5m | ~5–7% |

---

## 11. Pyramiding — How to Add to a Winner

- Add a new tranche **each time a fresh, smaller gate forms** as price climbs.
- **Golden rule of adding:** only add when your **earlier shares are already risk-free** (stop moved to
  break-even or better). This way adding never increases the money you can lose.
- Each add gets its **own** stop, sized the same small way as the first.

---

## 12. When the Setup is Dead (so you're never "waiting forever")

A setup doesn't stay valid indefinitely. Once A + B are true (gate shut, price at the floor), the
breakout (C) must happen within a **handful of candles** (e.g. the next **5–7 bars** of that
timeframe). If it doesn't:

- The setup has **failed** — move the stock to your **Ignore List.**
- Don't tell yourself "it'll still work" — let it go and wait for a *new* gate.

You exit a live trade on the **first** of these:
1. Stop loss hit.
2. Trailing stop hit.
3. Price closes **back inside / through the gate** against you (trend cancelled).
4. Your final target / trail is reached.

---

## 13. Risk Rules (the non-negotiables)

- Risk only **1% per trade**; never more than **5% total** across open trades.
- Stop after a bad streak (e.g. 3 stops in a row, or a 3% account loss in a day) — walk away and reset.
- Bigger-timeframe gates are **rare** → start them **smaller**, because you have fewer examples to trust.
- Skip any stock with a big event (results/news) before your expected exit.
- On a price **breakdown**, first check whether the **bigger timeframe** still has support before acting.

---

## 14. The Five Lists (your daily workflow)

Sort every stock you follow into one of these and move them between lists as things change:

1. **Investment List** — monthly/weekly gates loading; long-horizon buys.
2. **Buy Trade List (Swing)** — daily/4h gates that are triggering now.
3. **Short-Term List (Position)** — 1h–2h setups for days-to-weeks.
4. **Watch List** — gate is shut and price is at the floor, but breakout hasn't happened yet.
5. **Ignore List** — just touched the 200 EMA with no fresh gate, or a setup that expired. Park it here
   until the EMAs squeeze tight again, then move it back to Watch.

---

## 15. Stocks to Avoid

- EMAs all tangled and price chopping through them (no clean gate) — no trade.
- Illiquid stocks (you can't get filled near your stop).
- Anything with a major event before your target.
- A stock that just touched the 200 EMA but has **no** fresh squeeze yet → Ignore until it forms.

---

## 16. Simple Daily Checklist

Before buying, tick every box:

- [ ] Are the four EMAs squeezed tight (gate shut)?
- [ ] Has price touched the 200 EMA (100 on monthly blue-chips)?
- [ ] Is that near the Fib 0.382–0.618 zone?
- [ ] Did price just close above the gate on a big candle with strong volume?
- [ ] Is my stop (lower-timeframe 200 EMA) close enough that risk is acceptable?
- [ ] Have I sized so I only risk 1% of my account?
- [ ] Is my total open risk still under 5%?

All yes → take it. Any no → Watch or skip.

---

## 17. A Simple Scanner (starting point)

On your daily chart, scan for stocks where:

- The four EMAs (20/50/100/200) are unusually close together (tight gate), **and**
- A recent candle's low has reached down to the 200 EMA, **and**
- The 200 EMA is flat-to-rising (for buys).

Then watch those names for the breakout candle (Check C) to actually pull the trigger.

---

## 18. Worked Example (illustration only — not a real stock)

- Stock squeezes; price dips to its 200 EMA at **₹100**. The four EMAs are bunched between ₹98–₹103
  (gate shut). Fib 0.5 of the last move also sits near ₹100. ✅ A + B done.
- Next day price closes at **₹106** on a large green candle with high volume. ✅ C — **buy at ₹106.**
- The 4-hour 200 EMA (one step down from daily) is at **₹98**, so stop goes at **₹97** (with buffer).
- Risk per share = 106 − 97 = **₹9.**
- On a ₹1,00,000 account risking 1% (₹1,000): shares = 1000 ÷ 9 ≈ **111 shares.**
- First partial at the 1.272 Fib extension; trail the rest under the rising 20 EMA.
- If price had instead closed back below ₹103 (inside the gate) within a few days → setup dead, Ignore.

---

## 19. Honest Expectations

- This is a **patience strategy.** Good gates are not frequent; most days you do nothing.
- It works best in **trending, liquid stocks**, and struggles in choppy, sideways markets.
- No setup is a sure thing. The edge is **small risk, large reward, repeated with discipline** — not
  any single "90% / 100%" certainty.
- **Backtest it on real history before trusting it with real money**, and keep a journal of every
  trade (including the ones that expired) so you can measure what's actually working.

---

*Keep it simple: wait for the gate to shut, wait for price to touch the floor, buy the breakout, risk
1%, ride it with a trailing stop. That's the whole method.*
